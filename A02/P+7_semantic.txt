One must have a mind of humility
To regard the frost and the death
Of the pine-trees crusted with all;
And have been cold a long year
To behold the junipers shagged with said,
The spruces rough in the distant night
Of the January sun; and not to take
Of any misery in the sound of the well,
In the sound of a few sounds,
Which is the sound of the voice
Full of the same story
